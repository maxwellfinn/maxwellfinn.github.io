What Your CPA Gets Wrong About QSBS

You've probably heard of QSBS‚Äîthe Qualified Small Business Stock exclusion under Section 1202. Your accountant may have mentioned it. Maybe you've even assumed you have it handled.

Here's the quick version: Founders and early investors can exclude up to $15 million in capital gains (or 10x their cost basis, whichever is greater) from federal taxes when selling qualified stock.

That's significant. And most financially-savvy founders know about it.

But here's what most advisors fail to mention‚Äîthe detail that changes everything:

That $15 million limit isn't capped per company. It's capped per taxpayer.

Read that again. The exclusion isn't a single $15M ceiling. It's $15 million per qualifying taxpayer. Which means your potential exclusion can be multiplied‚Äîlegally‚Äîwith the right structure in place.

If you have $45 million in gains, you're not limited to excluding $15 million. With proper planning, you could potentially exclude the entire amount.

This isn't a gray area. It's not aggressive tax planning. It's how IRC Section 1202 was written.

The "Trust Stacking" Strategy That Multiplies Your Exclusion

The strategy is called QSBS "stacking." And while ultra-wealthy families and their private wealth advisors have used it for years, it's only recently become accessible to founders without $50,000 estate planning retainers.

Here's the core mechanism:

When you gift QSBS to a non-grantor irrevocable trust, that trust becomes its own taxpayer for federal income tax purposes. Which means it qualifies for its own $15 million exclusion.

The July 2025 Changes Make This More Valuable Than Ever

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 4, 2025, expanded QSBS significantly:

  • Exclusion cap raised from $10M to $15M per taxpayer
  • Asset threshold increased from $50M to $75M for qualifying corporations
  • New tiered schedule: 50% at 3 years, 75% at 4 years, 100% at 5 years
  • Inflation indexing begins after 2026

For stock issued after July 4, 2025, this means even greater opportunity. But it also means the window for establishing stacking structures has never been more valuable.
